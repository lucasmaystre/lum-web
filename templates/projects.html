Learning Preferences from Choices

Recently, Negahban, Oh and Shah proposed an elegant random-walk algorithm for aggregating pairwise comparisons into ranking, in the spirit of PageRank. In our work, we uncover a link between this spectral algorithm and maximum-likelihood estimation in a well-known statistical model. This link enables us to improve both computational and statistical efficiency of inference algorithms for models of the Plackett–Luce family. More details in our paper:

L. Maystre, M. Grossglauser, “Fast and Accurate Inference of Plackett–Luce Models,” NIPS, 2015.

How can one learn a ranking over items using as few comparisons as possible? We tackle this problem in Luce's model, when comparison outcomes are noisy and at times inconsistent with the the underlying ranking. It turns out that a simple strategy performs really well, both in theory and in practice. Read our preprint:

L. Maystre, M. Grossglauser, “Robust Active Ranking from Sparse Noisy Comparisons,” Feb. 2015, preprint, arXiv:1502.05556 [stat.ML].

Mobile Micro-measures

In the context of the Data for Development Challenge organized by Orange-France Telecom, we used data extracted from call detail records to generate personalized recommendations to help curb the spread of an infectious disease. The idea is that by considering the microscopic details of social interactions and mobility we can find clever ways to mitigate an epidemic. In particular, we aim to find mitigation strategies that have a minimal impact on daily life but significantly impact the epidemic spread.

Our work was presented at NetMob 2013 in Boston. The paper we submitted for the data challenge:

M. Kafsi, E. Kazemi, L. Maystre, L. Yartseva, M. Grossglauser, P. Thiran, “Mitigating Epidemics through Mobile Micro-measures,” Feb. 2013, preprint, arXiv:1307.2084 [cs.SI].

GroupStreamer

GroupStreamer is a music recommender system for groups of people. It was originally developped during my master's thesis, and released as an Android app on Google Play. The idea is to infer musical preferences based on the music present on users' mobile phones, and automatically generate a playlist based on the preferences of nearby people. All the code is available on GitHub. For more information, you can read my report:

L. Maystre, “Music Recommendation to Groups,” M.Sc. thesis, Ecole Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne, Switzerland, 2012.
