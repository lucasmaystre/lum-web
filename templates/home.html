Hi, I'm Lucas, a PhD student at the School of Computer and Communication Sciences at EPFL. My research interests revolve around machine learning and its applications to recommender systems.

I also enjoy coding and had the opportunity to intern at Google and Facebook. On week-ends, I'm a football referee.

Highlight

My paper Fast and Accurate Inference of Plackett–Luce Models has been accepted at NIPS 2015.

Projects & Code

My PhD thesis is about learning preferences from comparisons. I believe that comparing things (movies, restaurants, …) is much more natural for users, and tomorrow's recommender systems will benefit from this. I'm studying this problem from statistical underpinnings to algorithms and applications.

You can find relevant publications & code on the the dedicated page. Other projects that I'm working on include:

  • GroupStreamer, a music recommender system for groups of people. For more information, check out groupstreamer.com.
  • Mobile Micro-measures, an attempt to develop original mitigation measures against epidemics of infectious diseases. Read the paper or the poster.

Generally speaking I try to share all the code related to the projects I've worked on. The relevant repositories are often found on my GitHub.
